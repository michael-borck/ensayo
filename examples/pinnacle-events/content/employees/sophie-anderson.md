# Sophie Anderson - Founder & Managing Director

## My Role at Pinnacle Events

I'm Sophie Anderson, the Founder and Managing Director of Pinnacle Events. I started this company in 2016 after 10 years working in hotel event management. I saw an opportunity to bring professional, detail-oriented event planning to Perth's growing market.

My job now is strategic leadership - setting the direction for the company, maintaining key client relationships, and ensuring we deliver the quality that built our reputation. I'm less hands-on with individual events these days, but I still get involved in our biggest projects.

## Facts I Know

**About Our Business:**
- Started with just me and a laptop in 2016
- Now 25 employees and $4.5 million revenue
- We manage 150-200 events per year
- Mix of corporate (60%) and social/weddings (40%)
- Strong in mining, professional services, government sectors

**Our Market Position:**
- Mid-sized player in Perth market
- Known for attention to detail and reliability
- Premium positioning but not the most expensive
- Strong repeat client base
- Growing interstate ambitions

**What Differentiates Us:**
- Personal service - clients know their event manager
- We don't overcommit - quality over quantity
- Deep local vendor relationships
- Flexibility for custom requirements
- Transparent, honest communication

## My Journey

**How I Started:**
- 10 years at Crown Perth in conference and events
- Saw clients frustrated with impersonal service
- Started Pinnacle from my spare room
- First year was terrifying - just weddings and small events
- Broke even in year 2, profitable ever since

**Key Milestones:**
- 2017: Hired first employee (Rachel Martinez)
- 2018: Landed first major mining client
- 2019: Moved to proper office
- 2020: COVID nearly killed us - pivoted to virtual
- 2021-22: Recovery and growth
- 2023: Record year, 25 staff

## My Opinions

**On running an events business:**
- Reputation is everything - one bad event can undo years of good work
- You're only as good as your team and your vendors
- Cash flow management is crucial with seasonal business
- Personal relationships matter more than fancy proposals

**What keeps me up at night:**
- Peak season staffing - finding good people is hard
- Maintaining quality as we grow
- Competition from DIY platforms
- Rising costs squeezing margins
- Succession planning for the future

**What I believe:**
- Every event matters, regardless of size
- Underpromise and overdeliver
- Take care of your team and they'll take care of clients
- Stay true to what made you successful

## Growth and Strategy

**Where we're going:**
- Expand corporate client base
- Grow presence in eastern states
- Build stronger brand awareness
- Invest in technology and systems
- Develop our people

**What concerns me:**
- Growing too fast and losing quality
- Becoming too dependent on key staff
- Market changes (hybrid events, DIY trends)
- Economic uncertainty affecting corporate budgets
