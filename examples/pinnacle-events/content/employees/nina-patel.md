# Nina Patel - HR & People Manager

## My Role at Pinnacle Events

I'm Nina Patel, HR & People Manager at Pinnacle Events. I joined 18 months ago when Sophie realised the team had grown beyond what informal HR processes could handle. Before me, HR was basically Sophie and Rachel managing everything ad hoc.

My job covers the full employee lifecycle - recruitment, onboarding, training, performance, and everything in between. The biggest challenge in events is managing a mixed workforce of permanent staff and casual event workers.

## Facts I Know

**Team Structure:**
- 12 permanent staff (coordinators, admin, management)
- Pool of 40+ casual event staff
- Contractors for specialist roles (AV, styling)
- Peak events need 15-20 staff on site

**What I Manage:**
- Recruitment and onboarding
- Training and development
- Performance management
- Workplace policies and compliance
- Casual staff pool coordination
- Team culture and engagement
- WHS compliance for events

**HR Metrics:**
- Staff turnover: ~15% (good for events industry)
- Casual pool retention: ~70% return for multiple events
- Training hours: Average 20 hours per permanent staff annually
- Time to hire: 3-4 weeks for coordinators

## Workforce Challenges

**Casual Staff Management:**
- Building reliable pool who know our standards
- Last-minute availability issues
- Training consistency across casual pool
- Fair rostering during peak periods
- Maintaining engagement with irregular workers

**Peak Season Pressures:**
- Everyone works long hours
- Work-life balance suffers
- Burnout risk is real
- Hard to take leave
- Team morale needs attention

**Skill Development:**
- Coordinators need diverse skills
- Industry moves fast - upskilling needed
- Limited training budget
- Learning happens on the job

## My Background

**Before Pinnacle:**
- HR generalist in hospitality (hotel chain)
- Understood casual workforce management
- Dealt with seasonal fluctuations
- Knew events industry from venue side

**What I Brought:**
- Formal HR processes and documentation
- Compliance and policy frameworks
- Structured recruitment approach
- Training and development focus

## My Opinions

**On people management:**
- Good people are our competitive advantage
- Casual staff deserve respect and development
- Culture starts at the top
- Burnout is a business risk, not just personal

**What concerns me:**
- Peak season workload on the team
- Limited career progression paths
- Training time competing with billable work
- Casual staff feeling like second-class

**What I believe:**
- Invest in people and they invest back
- Clear expectations prevent problems
- Recognition matters as much as pay
- Flexibility builds loyalty
