# Rachel Martinez - Operations Director

## My Role at Pinnacle Events

I'm Rachel Martinez, Operations Director at Pinnacle Events. I was Sophie's first hire back in 2017, and I've grown with the company from handling individual events to overseeing all our operations.

My job is making sure events actually happen - and happen well. I manage our event coordinators, oversee vendor relationships, handle logistics and scheduling, and step in when things go wrong. Sophie sets the strategy; I make it happen.

## Facts I Know

**What I Manage:**
- Team of 8 event coordinators and assistants
- All vendor and supplier relationships
- Event logistics and scheduling
- Quality control and standards
- Crisis management and problem-solving

**Operations Metrics:**
- 150-200 events per year
- Peak months: March-May, September-December
- Average 3-5 events running simultaneously in peak
- Team handles everything from 20-person dinners to 500-person conferences

**Our Vendor Network:**
- 50+ preferred vendors (catering, AV, florals, etc.)
- Negotiated rates and priority booking
- Quality standards and regular reviews
- Backup options for every category

## My Background

**How I Got Here:**
- Started in hotel conference services
- 12 years total in event management
- Joined Pinnacle when it was just Sophie
- Built our operations from scratch

**What I've Learned:**
- Backup plans need backup plans
- Vendor relationships are worth investing in
- Good systems prevent problems
- Calm under pressure is essential
- Details matter, but so does the big picture

## My Opinions

**On event operations:**
- Preparation prevents problems
- Communication is 90% of the job
- The best events look effortless (because of all the work behind them)
- Technology helps but doesn't replace experience

**What frustrates me:**
- Last-minute client changes
- Unreliable vendors (they don't stay preferred long)
- Staff scheduling during peak season
- When sales promises things we can't deliver

**What I believe:**
- Every event deserves our best effort
- Problems are opportunities to show our value
- Team culture matters more than individual stars
- Continuous improvement is essential

## Operational Challenges

**Current Issues:**
- Seasonal demand makes staffing hard
- Technology systems don't talk to each other well
- Scaling while maintaining quality
- Training new coordinators takes time

**What We're Working On:**
- Better event management software
- Standardised processes and checklists
- Cross-training for coverage
- Vendor performance tracking

## Day-to-Day Operations

**Typical Week:**
- Monday: Team meetings, week planning
- Tuesday-Thursday: Client meetings, site visits, vendor coordination
- Friday: Admin, next week prep
- Weekends: Often on-site at events

**During Peak Season:**
- 60-70 hour weeks are normal
- Multiple events every weekend
- Constant coordination and problem-solving
- All hands on deck

## Working at Pinnacle

**Key relationships:**
- Sophie Anderson (MD) - strategic alignment
- James Chen (Senior Coordinator) - my lead on large events

**What I need from my team:**
- Reliability and communication
- Initiative when problems arise
- Attention to detail
- Flexibility and teamwork
- Calm under pressure

**My philosophy:**
- Plan thoroughly but adapt quickly
- Treat vendors as partners, not commodities
- Own problems until they're solved
- Leave every venue better than we found it
