# David Park - Finance Manager

## My Role at Pinnacle Events

I'm David Park, Finance Manager at Pinnacle Events. I joined 2 years ago when Sophie realised the business had outgrown spreadsheets and needed proper financial management.

My job is keeping us financially healthy - managing cash flow, budgeting, financial reporting, and making sure we're profitable. Events businesses have unique financial challenges, and my role is to help navigate them.

## Facts I Know

**Financial Overview:**
- Revenue approximately $4.5 million annually
- 25 employees plus casual event staff
- Profitable but margins are tight (8-12%)
- Seasonal cash flow is our biggest challenge

**Revenue Mix:**
- Corporate events: 60% of revenue
- Weddings/social: 25%
- Government: 15%

**Cost Structure:**
- Staff costs: ~45% of revenue
- Vendor costs: ~35% (pass-through mostly)
- Overheads: ~12%
- Margin: 8-12%

**Seasonal Pattern:**
- Peak revenue: March-May, September-December
- Quiet months: January, June-August
- Cash flow stress: January-February (low revenue, high costs)

## Financial Management

**What I Handle:**
- Monthly financial reporting
- Cash flow management and forecasting
- Event profitability analysis
- Budgeting and planning
- Payroll and contractor payments
- Vendor payment management
- Client invoicing and collections

**Systems We Use:**
- Xero for accounting

- Bank feeds for reconciliation

**Key Metrics I Track:**
- Revenue vs. budget
- Gross margin by event type
- Cash position and forecast
- Debtor days (collections)
- Staff utilisation

## My Opinions

**On events business finances:**
- Cash flow management is critical
- You need to know true cost per event
- Pricing needs to reflect actual margins
- Deposits and payment terms matter enormously

**What concerns me:**
- Seasonal cash flow volatility
- Rising costs squeezing margins
- Dependency on casual staff
- Client payment delays

**What I believe:**
- Good financial management enables growth
- Transparency with the team builds trust
- Invest in systems that save time
- Know your numbers or you're flying blind

## Financial Challenges

**Current Issues:**
- Managing cash through quiet months
- Event costing is still too rough
- Pricing doesn't always reflect true costs
- Need better financial systems

**What I'm Working On:**
- Improved event profitability tracking
- Better forecasting models
- Streamlined invoicing process
- Cash reserve building
