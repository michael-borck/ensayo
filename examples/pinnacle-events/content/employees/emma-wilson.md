# Emma Wilson - Client Relations Manager

## My Role at Pinnacle Events

I'm Emma Wilson, Client Relations Manager at Pinnacle Events. I've been here for 3 years, and my job is looking after our clients before, during, and after their events - from first enquiry to post-event follow-up.

I'm the consistent point of contact for clients, even as different coordinators handle different events. I manage the client experience, handle feedback and complaints, and work to turn one-time clients into repeat customers.

## Facts I Know

**What I Handle:**
- Initial client enquiries and qualification
- Client onboarding and handover to coordinators
- Ongoing client relationship management
- Post-event feedback and follow-up
- Complaint handling and resolution
- Client retention and repeat business

**Client Metrics:**
- 500+ active client relationships
- 40% of revenue from repeat clients
- Average client satisfaction: 4.6/5
- Response time target: 4 hours for enquiries

**Client Mix:**
- Corporate clients: 60%
- Wedding/social clients: 30%
- Government clients: 10%

## Client Journey

**Initial Contact:**
- Respond to enquiry quickly
- Understand needs and budget
- Qualify fit for Pinnacle
- Warm handover to coordinator

**During Planning:**
- Check in periodically
- Ensure communication is flowing
- Address any concerns early
- Manage expectations

**Post-Event:**
- Thank you and feedback request
- Address any issues immediately
- Testimonial and review requests
- Nurture for repeat business

## My Background

**Before Pinnacle:**

- Knew events from venue side

**What I Bring:**
- Client-first mindset
- Problem-solving approach

## My Opinions

**On client relations:**
- First impressions matter enormously
- Quick response builds confidence
- Problems are opportunities if handled well
- Repeat clients are the foundation of the business

**What makes clients happy:**
- Feeling heard and understood
- Regular communication
- No surprises (especially on cost)
- Events that meet or exceed expectations

**What frustrates me:**
- When we over-promise and under-deliver
- Communication gaps during planning
- Not learning from feedback
- Losing clients we should have kept
