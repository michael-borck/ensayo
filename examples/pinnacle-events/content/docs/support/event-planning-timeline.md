# Event Planning Timelines - Pinnacle Events

## Recommended Lead Times

### Large Corporate Events (200+ guests)
- **Ideal**: 6-12 months
- **Minimum**: 3-4 months
- Complex logistics, venue availability, and vendor coordination require longer lead times

### Conferences and Multi-day Events
- **Ideal**: 9-18 months
- **Minimum**: 6 months
- Speaker booking, attendee marketing, and sponsorship development need time

### Medium Corporate Events (50-200 guests)
- **Ideal**: 3-6 months
- **Minimum**: 6-8 weeks
- Standard venues and vendors more accessible

### Weddings
- **Ideal**: 12-18 months
- **Minimum**: 6 months
- Popular venues and vendors book well in advance, especially in peak season

### Small Private Events (<50 guests)
- **Ideal**: 2-3 months
- **Minimum**: 4 weeks
- More flexibility but quality still requires planning

## Planning Milestones

### 6+ Months Out
- [ ] Brief and proposal signed
- [ ] Venue secured
- [ ] Key vendors shortlisted
- [ ] High-level budget confirmed
- [ ] Save-the-dates sent (if applicable)

### 3-4 Months Out
- [ ] All major vendors booked
- [ ] Menu planning underway
- [ ] Theme/styling direction confirmed
- [ ] Invitations designed
- [ ] Registration system set up (if applicable)

### 6-8 Weeks Out
- [ ] Run sheet draft complete
- [ ] Final vendor confirmations
- [ ] Menu finalised
- [ ] Seating plan started
- [ ] AV and technical requirements confirmed

### 2-3 Weeks Out
- [ ] Final guest numbers
- [ ] Run sheet signed off
- [ ] Final payments processed
- [ ] Vendor briefing documents sent
- [ ] Contingency plans confirmed

### 1 Week Out
- [ ] Final confirmations to all vendors
- [ ] Team briefing complete
- [ ] Client pre-event meeting
- [ ] Site visit and walk-through
- [ ] Emergency contacts distributed

### Day Before
- [ ] Venue setup (if permitted)
- [ ] Equipment checks
- [ ] Final run sheet distribution
- [ ] Weather contingency confirmation (if outdoor)
- [ ] Team ready and rested

## Peak Season Considerations

### High Demand Periods (Perth)
- March to May (autumn corporate season)
- September to December (spring/summer events, Christmas)
- October to April (wedding season)

### Lower Demand Periods
- January (summer holidays)
- June to August (winter)

During peak periods:
- Book venues 9-12 months ahead
- Confirm key vendors early
- Expect higher pricing
- Less flexibility for last-minute changes

## Rush Event Planning

For events with less than minimum lead time:
- Limited venue and vendor options
- Potential premium pricing
- Simplified scope recommended
- Daily communication required
- Additional contingency planning

We can deliver quality events in compressed timelines but set realistic expectations with clients about what's achievable.
