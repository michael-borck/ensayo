# Stakeholder Communication

## Communication as Core Competency

In event management, communication is the job:
- Clients need confidence and updates
- Vendors need clear briefs and coordination
- Team needs direction and information
- Problems need quick escalation and resolution

Poor communication is the root cause of most event issues.

## Stakeholder Mapping

### Client Stakeholders
| Role | Communication Needs | Frequency |
|------|---------------------|-----------|
| Primary decision maker | Progress, decisions, budget | Weekly minimum |
| Day-of contact | Logistics, run sheet, details | Increases near event |
| Finance/procurement | Invoicing, contracts | As needed |
| Other influencers | Inclusion, updates | Periodic |

### Vendor Stakeholders
| Role | Communication Needs | Frequency |
|------|---------------------|-----------|
| Venue manager | Logistics, timeline, requirements | Key milestones |
| Catering manager | Numbers, dietary, service style | Multiple touchpoints |
| AV supplier | Technical requirements, run sheet | Detailed pre-event |
| Other vendors | Brief, confirmation, on-day | As needed |

### Internal Stakeholders
| Role | Communication Needs | Frequency |
|------|---------------------|-----------|
| Event coordinator | Everything | Continuous |
| Operations Director | Status, issues, resources | Regular check-ins |
| Support team | Their specific tasks | Task-based |
| Casual staff | Briefing, instructions | Pre-event, on-day |

## Communication Principles

### Be Proactive
- Update before they ask
- Flag issues early
- Share good news promptly
- Anticipate questions

### Be Clear
- Simple language
- Specific details
- Confirm understanding
- Document decisions

### Be Consistent
- Regular rhythm of updates
- Same format each time
- One source of truth
- Follow through on promises

### Be Responsive
- Acknowledge quickly
- Provide timeline if can't answer immediately
- Close the loop on requests
- Don't leave people waiting

## Communication Formats

### Email Best Practices
**Subject Lines:**
- Include event name and topic
- "ABC Company Gala - Menu Confirmation Required"
- "Smith Wedding - Weekly Update #4"

**Structure:**
- Key message/action first
- Supporting details below
- Clear call to action
- Deadline if applicable

**Professional Standards:**
- Reply within one business day
- CC sparingly and appropriately
- Proofread before sending
- Professional signature

### Phone/Video Calls
**When to Use:**
- Complex discussions
- Sensitive topics
- Building relationships
- Urgent matters

**Best Practices:**
- Have an agenda
- Take notes
- Summarise decisions
- Follow up in writing

### Messaging/Chat
**When to Use:**
- Quick questions
- Time-sensitive updates
- Team coordination
- Informal check-ins

**Best Practices:**
- Respect response time expectations
- Don't use for complex discussions
- Keep professional
- Move to email for documentation

## Client Communication Plan

### Planning Phase (Ongoing)
- Weekly email update
- Fortnightly call/meeting
- Milestone celebrations
- Issue escalation as needed

### Final Month
- Twice-weekly updates
- Detailed logistics communication
- Confirmation requests
- Run sheet reviews

### Final Week
- Daily check-in option
- Final confirmation calls
- Emergency contact exchange
- Detailed briefings

### Event Day
- Pre-event check-in
- Regular status updates (if appropriate)
- Immediate issue communication
- Post-event brief thanks

### Post-Event
- Thank you within 24 hours
- Feedback request at 48-72 hours
- Follow-up on any issues
- Testimonial request (timing matters)

## Vendor Communication

### Briefing Documents
Every vendor receives:
- Event overview and objectives
- Their specific requirements
- Timeline and run sheet excerpt
- Contact details
- Logistics (load-in, parking, etc.)
- Emergency procedures

### Confirmation Protocol
- Initial confirmation at booking
- Reminder at 2 weeks
- Final confirmation at 48 hours
- Day-before check-in for critical vendors

### On-Day Coordination
- Single point of contact
- Clear escalation path
- Issue resolution authority
- Post-event feedback

## Managing Difficult Communications

### Delivering Bad News
1. Be direct but compassionate
2. Explain the situation clearly
3. Present solutions or options
4. Take responsibility where appropriate
5. Follow up to ensure resolution

### Handling Client Concerns
1. Listen fully before responding
2. Acknowledge their feelings
3. Clarify the facts
4. Propose resolution
5. Follow through completely
6. Document the interaction

### Escalation Framework
| Issue Level | Who Handles | Client Notification |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Minor (no impact) | Coordinator | Not needed |
| Moderate (managed impact) | Coordinator | Inform after resolution |
| Significant (noticeable impact) | + Operations Director | Inform promptly |
| Critical (event at risk) | + Managing Director | Immediate communication |

## Crisis Communication

### During Event Emergencies
1. Ensure safety first
2. Assess situation quickly
3. Communicate to client (brief, factual)
4. Implement contingency
5. Keep stakeholders updated
6. Document everything

### Post-Incident
1. Client debrief promptly
2. Acknowledge and apologise if appropriate
3. Explain what happened and response
4. Outline any remediation
5. Follow up to confirm satisfaction
