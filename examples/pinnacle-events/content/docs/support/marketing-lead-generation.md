# Marketing and Lead Generation

## Events Industry Marketing Challenges

Marketing event management services differs from product marketing:
- **Intangible service**: Can't show the product before purchase
- **High consideration**: Significant investment, emotional stakes
- **Trust-based**: Clients hand over important occasions
- **Portfolio-dependent**: Past work is the best proof
- **Relationship-driven**: Referrals dominate

## Pinnacle Events Marketing Mix

### Current Channel Performance
| Channel | % of Leads | Conversion Rate | Cost per Lead |
|---------|------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Referrals | 60% | 45% | Low (relationship investment) |
| Website/SEO | 20% | 25% | Medium |
| Social media | 10% | 15% | Low-Medium |
| Directories | 5% | 20% | Medium |
| Paid advertising | 5% | 10% | High |

### Key Insight
Referrals convert at nearly double other channels. Marketing strategy should support and amplify referrals, not replace them.

## Brand Positioning

### Pinnacle's Position
- **Target**: Clients who want quality and personal service
- **Position**: Boutique event management with big-company capability
- **Differentiation**: Personal touch, attention to detail, reliability
- **Price position**: Premium but not luxury

### Brand Messaging
- "Creating Unforgettable Moments"
- Professional, warm, trustworthy
- Emphasis on reducing client stress
- Showcase real events and real outcomes

### Brand Voice
- Confident but not arrogant
- Warm but professional
- Knowledgeable but accessible
- Enthusiastic but not over-the-top

## Segment-Specific Marketing

### Corporate Marketing
**Target**: EA/PAs, marketing managers, HR managers
**Channels**: LinkedIn, direct outreach, industry networking
**Content**: Case studies, ROI focus, reliability emphasis
**Message**: "We make you look good"

**Tactics:**
- LinkedIn company page and personal profiles
- Industry event attendance
- Direct approach to target companies
- Corporate testimonials and case studies
- Proposal templates for fast response

### Wedding Marketing
**Target**: Engaged couples, typically 25-40
**Channels**: Instagram, wedding directories, venue partnerships
**Content**: Beautiful imagery, emotional stories, styled shoots
**Message**: "Your perfect day, stress-free"

**Tactics:**
- Instagram presence (visual platform)
- Wedding directory listings (Easy Weddings, etc.)
- Styled shoots for portfolio
- Venue partnership referrals
- Wedding expos and open days

## Lead Generation Strategies

### Referral Amplification
- Ask for referrals at right moment (post-event high)
- Make referring easy (provide materials)
- Thank and recognise referrers
- Nurture referral sources (venues, vendors)
- Track referral sources

### Content Marketing
- Blog posts on event planning topics
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Real wedding/event features
- Seasonal planning guides
- Downloadable checklists

### Search Engine Optimisation
- Local SEO (Perth events, WA weddings)
- Service-specific pages
- Venue partnership pages
- Blog content for long-tail keywords
- Google Business optimisation

### Social Media Strategy
**Instagram** (primary for weddings):
- 3-4 posts per week
- Stories for behind-the-scenes
- Reels for engagement
- Consistent aesthetic
- Vendor tagging for reach

**LinkedIn** (primary for corporate):
- 2-3 posts per week
- Thought leadership content
- Company updates and wins
- Team profiles active
- Engage with target companies

### Partnership Marketing
- Venue partnerships (mutual referrals)
- Vendor network cross-promotion
- Industry association involvement
- Complementary business referrals

## Lead Management

### Lead Qualification
**Budget**: Can they afford our services?
**Authority**: Are they the decision maker?
**Need**: Do they need what we offer?
**Timeline**: Is the timing realistic?

### Lead Scoring
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Referral from past client | +30 |
| Corporate enquiry | +20 |
| Realistic budget indicated | +20 |
| Event date confirmed | +15 |
| Responded to follow-up | +10 |
| Wedding enquiry | +10 |
| Vague requirements | -10 |
| Unrealistic budget | -20 |

### Lead Nurture Sequence
**Day 0**: Initial response (within 4 hours)
**Day 1**: Follow-up if no response
**Day 3**: Value-add content (checklist, guide)
**Day 7**: Check-in call/email
**Day 14**: Case study or testimonial share
**Day 30**: Final follow-up or nurture list

## Proposal and Conversion

### Proposal Best Practices
- Respond quickly (within 48 hours)
- Customise to their specific needs
- Show understanding of their vision
- Include relevant case studies
- Clear pricing structure
- Professional presentation

### Conversion Tactics
- Discovery call before proposal
- Site visit for serious enquiries
- Testimonials at right moment
- Address objections proactively
- Create urgency (availability)

## Marketing Metrics

### Key Performance Indicators
- Leads per month (target: 15-20 qualified)
- Conversion rate (target: 30%)
- Cost per lead by channel
- Time to respond
- Proposal win rate
- Referral rate

### Reporting
- Weekly: Lead activity summary
- Monthly: Full funnel analysis
- Quarterly: Channel performance review
- Annually: Strategy assessment

## Budget Allocation

### Current Marketing Budget
Approximately 3-4% of revenue

### Allocation
| Category | % of Budget |
|----------|-------------|
| Website and SEO | 25% |
| Social media (content, tools) | 20% |
| Photography | 20% |
| Directories and listings | 15% |
| Networking and events | 15% |

### ROI Focus
Limited budget means every dollar must work. Focus on:
- High-converting channels (referrals, SEO)
- Low-cost high-impact (social media)
- Long-term assets (photography, website)
