# Casual Workforce Management

## The Events Industry Workforce Model

Event management companies operate with a mixed workforce model:
- **Core permanent team**: Coordinators, managers, admin (handle planning)
- **Casual pool**: Event staff deployed as needed (handle execution)
- **Contractors**: Specialists for specific needs (AV techs, stylists)

This model provides flexibility but creates unique management challenges.

## Pinnacle Events Casual Pool

**Current Structure:**
- Pool of 40+ trained casual staff
- Mix of students, hospitality workers, career event staff
- Classified by skill level and reliability rating
- Called based on event requirements

**Typical Event Staffing:**
- Small event (50 guests): 3-5 casual staff
- Medium event (150 guests): 8-12 casual staff
- Large event (300+ guests): 15-20 casual staff

## Recruitment and Selection

### Finding Casual Staff
- Referrals from existing pool (preferred)
- Hospitality training programs
- University career services
- Industry job boards

### Selection Criteria
- Availability and flexibility
- Presentation and communication
- Relevant experience (hospitality, customer service)
- Physical capability (events involve standing, lifting)
- Reliability indicators

### Trial Process
- First shift supervised and assessed
- Feedback provided immediately
- Three successful shifts for full pool inclusion
- Ongoing assessment at each event

## Training and Development

### Induction Training (4 hours)
- Pinnacle standards and expectations
- WHS basics for events
- Common event types and roles
- Communication and escalation
- Uniform and presentation standards

### On-Event Training
- Pre-event briefing (30-60 minutes)
- Role-specific instructions
- Venue orientation
- Shadowing for new tasks

### Development Pathways
- Team leader opportunities for proven staff
- Specialist roles (registration, VIP liaison)
- Pathway to permanent positions
- Industry certifications supported

## Rostering and Communication

### Rostering Process
1. Event requirements confirmed (2-3 weeks out)
2. Availability request sent to suitable pool members
3. Roster finalised (1 week out)
4. Confirmation and briefing details sent
5. Reminder 48 hours before event

### Communication Channels
- Group messaging app for availability and updates
- Email for formal confirmations
- Phone for urgent changes
- Pre-event briefings in person

### Shift Management
- Check-in/check-out tracking
- Break management for long events
- Overtime monitoring
- Issue escalation to coordinator

## Performance Management

### Informal Feedback
- Brief feedback after every shift
- Recognition for good performance
- Immediate correction of issues
- Coordinator notes in system

### Formal Assessment
- Rating system (1-5) updated after events
- Reliability score tracked
- Skills and preferences recorded
- Annual review for regular casuals

### Performance Issues
- First instance: Verbal feedback on site
- Pattern emerging: Formal conversation
- Continued issues: Reduced roster priority
- Serious issues: Removal from pool

## Engagement and Retention

### Keeping Good Casuals
- Reliable rostering for those who want it
- Fair distribution of desirable shifts
- Recognition and thanks
- Team inclusion (invited to social events)
- Career pathway conversations

### Engagement Challenges
- Irregular work makes connection difficult
- Casuals may feel "second class"
- Limited training investment
- Competition from other employers

### Pinnacle Initiatives
- Casual staff newsletter
- End-of-season thank you event
- Bonus for peak season availability
- First offer for permanent roles

## Legal and Compliance

### Employment Classification
- Casual employment agreements required
- Minimum engagement periods
- Loading in lieu of leave entitlements
- Clear about no guaranteed hours

### WHS Obligations
- Same duty of care as permanent staff
- Appropriate training for tasks
- Safe systems of work
- Incident reporting applies

### Fair Work Considerations
- Casual conversion rights (after 12 months regular work)
- Roster changes with adequate notice
- Consultation on major changes
- Record keeping requirements

## Metrics and Reporting

**Key Metrics:**
- Pool size vs event needs
- Fill rate (% of shifts filled from first request)
- Reliability rate (% attended vs confirmed)
- Retention rate (% returning for multiple events)
- Training completion rates
- Incident rates

**Reporting:**
- Monthly casual workforce summary
- Post-event staffing review
- Quarterly pool health assessment
